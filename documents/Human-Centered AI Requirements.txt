# Document 6: Human-Centered AI Requirements for Codex

## Project

RiceCare AI  
Pest and Disease Advisory for Rice Farmers

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines the Human-Centered AI requirements for the RiceCare AI prototype.

Codex must build the prototype so that Human-Centered AI principles are visible in the actual app screens.

The app must not only look good. It must clearly show:

- Explainability
- Human control
- Human override and appeal
- Privacy and consent
- Bias and risk awareness
- Accessibility
- Governance and audit trail
- Safe advisory language

These requirements are important because RiceCare AI is not just a normal mobile app. It is an AI decision-support prototype for rice farmers.

---

# 1. Main Human-Centered AI Rule

RiceCare AI must support the farmer. It must not replace the farmer or the agricultural technician.

The AI must only suggest possible pest or disease issues.

The app must always communicate that:

```text
RiceCare AI provides advisory support only. It is not a final expert diagnosis.
````

The user must always have control over what happens next.

---

# 2. AI Autonomy Level

RiceCare AI should behave as a low-autonomy advisory system.

The AI can:

```text
Analyze a plant image using mock logic
Suggest a possible issue
Show confidence score
Show risk level
Explain visible symptoms
Suggest recommended actions
Recommend technician review
```

The AI must not:

```text
Make a final diagnosis
Force treatment action
Automatically apply decisions
Tell the farmer to use chemicals immediately
Hide uncertainty
Remove user control
Replace technician confirmation
```

The correct behavior is:

```text
AI suggests.
Farmer reviews.
Technician confirmation is available.
Farmer decides the next action.
```

---

# 3. Explainability Requirements

## Goal

The user must understand why the AI gave a result.

The result screen must not only show a disease or pest name. It must explain the basis of the suggestion.

## Required UI Elements

The AI Result screen must show:

```text
Possible Issue
Confidence Score
Risk Level
Category: Disease / Pest / Healthy / Needs Review
Image preview
Detected symptom areas
Why this result?
Detected symptoms list
Alternative possible results
Advisory disclaimer
Risk awareness note
```

## Required Explanation Pattern

Use this structure:

```text
Possible Issue: Rice Blast Disease
Confidence: 86%
Risk Level: High

Why this result?
The AI detected leaf spots, irregular brown lesions, and damaged leaf areas that may match symptoms of Rice Blast.
```

## Required Detected Symptoms Section

Show symptoms as short readable items.

Example:

```text
Detected symptoms:
- Leaf spots
- Irregular brown lesions
- Damaged leaf areas
```

## Required Alternative Results Section

Show top alternatives.

Example:

```text
Other possible matches:
- Brown Spot — 9%
- Bacterial Leaf Blight — 4%
- Healthy Plant — 1%
```

## Important Rule

Do not make the result look final.

Use:

```text
Possible Issue
May match symptoms
Advisory result
Other possible matches
Ask technician for confirmation
```

Avoid:

```text
Confirmed Disease
Final Diagnosis
The AI is sure
Treatment required
```

---

# 4. Confidence Score Requirements

## Goal

The confidence score must help the user understand uncertainty.

## Required Display

Show confidence as:

```text
86% Confidence
```

Use a progress bar or circular indicator.

## Confidence Interpretation

Use these rules:

```text
85–100%: High confidence
70–84%: Moderate confidence
50–69%: Low confidence
Below 50%: Uncertain / Needs Review
```

## Required Caution Text

Even when confidence is high, show:

```text
A high confidence score does not mean the result is always final.
```

## Low Confidence Behavior

If confidence is low or uncertain:

The result screen must say:

```text
The image does not clearly match one known pest or disease. Please retake the photo or ask a technician for confirmation.
```

Primary actions should become:

```text
Retake Photo
Ask Technician
```

Do not make “View Recommendations” the only strong action for low-confidence results.

---

# 5. Risk Level Requirements

## Goal

Risk level should help the farmer know how urgent the issue may be.

## Required Risk Levels

Use these labels:

```text
Low Risk
Medium Risk
High Risk
Needs Review
```

## Risk Behavior

### Low Risk

Use for healthy or minor advisory result.

Message:

```text
Continue regular field monitoring.
```

### Medium Risk

Use for possible pest damage or moderate disease concern.

Message:

```text
Monitor the affected plants and ask a technician if symptoms spread.
```

### High Risk

Use for serious disease-like symptoms.

Message:

```text
Ask a technician before applying chemical treatment, especially when many plants are affected.
```

### Needs Review

Use for uncertain result.

Message:

```text
The result is unclear. Retake the photo or ask a technician before taking action.
```

## Important Rule

Risk level must never pressure the user into unsafe action.

Do not say:

```text
Apply fungicide now.
Use pesticide immediately.
Disease confirmed.
```

Use safer wording:

```text
Consult an agricultural technician before applying chemical treatment.
```

---

# 6. Human Override Requirements

## Goal

The farmer must always be able to disagree with the AI.

## Required Actions on AI Result Screen

Every AI result screen must include:

```text
View Recommendations
Ask Technician
Report Wrong Result
Retake Photo
```

For low-confidence result, prioritize:

```text
Retake Photo
Ask Technician
```

For healthy result, include:

```text
Save Result
Scan Another Plant
Ask Technician if Needed
```

## Required Human Override Flow

```text
AI Result
→ Ask Technician
→ Technician Support Form
→ Review Submitted
→ Review Status
```

## Required Report Wrong Result Flow

```text
AI Result
→ Report Wrong Result
→ Submit Report
→ Review Submitted
```

## Required Wording

Use:

```text
If you think the AI result is incorrect or uncertain, you can request help from an agricultural technician.
```

Also use:

```text
Human review helps avoid relying only on AI.
```

## Important Rule

Do not hide the override options below too much content. They must be easy to find.

---

# 7. Appeal and Error Reporting Requirements

## Goal

The user must be able to challenge the AI result.

## Required Report Wrong Result Screen

The screen must include:

```text
Title: Report Wrong Result

Text:
Tell us why the result seems incorrect. This helps improve review and future advisory support.
```

Required fields:

```text
What seems wrong?
What did you observe in the field?
Location / Barangay optional
```

Required actions:

```text
Submit Report
Ask Technician Instead
Back to Result
```

## Required Submitted State

After reporting, show:

```text
Your request has been submitted for technician review.
```

Status:

```text
Under Review
```

## Important Rule

The report flow should not blame the user or make the user feel wrong for questioning the AI.

Use calm wording.

# 8. Privacy and Consent Requirements

## Goal

The app must ask permission before using plant images.

## Required Consent Screen

The consent screen must appear before the user reaches the main app.

Required title:

```text
Before You Start
```

Required explanation:

```text
RiceCare AI analyzes plant images to suggest possible pest or disease problems. Your image is used only for analysis and advisory support.
```

Required consent checklist:

```text
I understand this is advisory support, not a final expert diagnosis.
I allow the system to analyze the uploaded plant image.
I understand I can ask a technician for confirmation.
```

Required actions:

```text
I Agree and Continue
Learn about privacy
```

## Required Behavior

The continue button should appear disabled until consent is accepted.

This can be simulated with React state.

## Required Privacy Details

The app should explain:

```text
How your image is used
What is saved
Optional sharing with technician
Deleting saved scan history
Advisory limitation
```

## Required Manage Consent Screen

The settings section must include Manage Consent.

Consent options:

```text
Allow image analysis
Save scan results
Allow technician sharing
Allow anonymized image use for improvement
```

## Important Rule

Optional sharing must not look forced.

Use this note:

```text
You can use advisory analysis without allowing optional data sharing.
```

---

# 9. Data Minimization Requirements

## Goal

The prototype should show that only necessary information is collected.

## Required Behavior

Do not ask for unnecessary personal information.

Technician support form may ask for:

```text
Farmer Name
Location / Barangay
Short Description of Problem
Contact Number optional
```

Do not ask for:

```text
Exact home address
Government ID
Password
Payment information
Sensitive personal details
```

## Required Privacy Wording

Use:

```text
Plant images are used for advisory analysis. Saved results can be deleted by the user.
```

Use:

```text
Contact number is optional and only used if a technician needs more information.
```

---

# 10. Bias and Risk Awareness Requirements

## Goal

The app must explain that AI accuracy can be affected by real-world conditions.

## Required Risk Awareness Note

Use this note where appropriate:

```text
Image quality, lighting, crop variety, and local field conditions may affect AI accuracy.
```

Place this note on:

```text
Consent screen
Scan screen
Image preview screen
AI result screen
Help article
About screen
```

## Required Poor Image Warning

If the image may be unclear, show:

```text
This photo may be too blurry, dark, or far from the affected plant area.
```

Actions:

```text
Retake Photo
View Photo Guide
Continue Anyway
```

## Required Unknown / Uncertain Handling

For uncertain results:

```text
The image does not clearly match one known pest or disease.
```

Then recommend:

```text
Retake the photo.
Ask a technician.
Do not take treatment action yet.
```

## Important Rule

Do not force a known disease label when the result is uncertain.

---

# 11. Accessibility Requirements

## Goal

The app must be usable for farmers with low digital literacy and different accessibility needs.

## Required Accessibility Features

The Settings screen must include:

```text
Text Size: Normal / Large
Language: English / Filipino / Local Language
Voice Guidance: On / Off
High Contrast Mode: On / Off
Simple Mode: On / Off
```

## Required Visual Behavior

Large Text:

```text
Increase readable text size.
Increase button text slightly.
Keep layout usable.
```

High Contrast Mode:

```text
Make text darker.
Make borders stronger.
Improve contrast between cards and background.
```

Voice Guidance:

```text
Toggle should switch on/off.
No real audio is required unless easy to implement.
```

Simple Mode:

```text
Reduce non-essential helper text.
Keep warnings and safety notes visible.
```

Language:

```text
Allow selection, but full translation is not required for the prototype.
```

## Accessibility Design Rules

Codex must ensure:

```text
Buttons are large enough to tap.
Important text is not too small.
Color is not the only way to show meaning.
Risk labels include words, not only colors.
Icons have text labels.
Screens are scrollable when needed.
```

## Required Screens with Audit Trail

Audit information must appear on:

```text
History and Audit Log
Result Details
Review Status
```

## Required History Card Data

Each saved result should show:

```text
Date
Possible Issue
Confidence
Risk Level
Status
View Details
```

## Required Result Details Audit Section

Example:

```text
Audit Detail:
- Image uploaded
- AI analysis completed

- Recommendation opened
- Technician review requested

# 14. Safe Advisory Language Requirements

## Goal

The app must avoid unsafe or overconfident advice.

## Use These Words

```text
Possible issue
May match symptoms
Advisory support
Ask technician
Human confirmation
Risk level
Confidence score
Recommended practice
Prevention tips
```

## Avoid These Words

```text
Confirmed diagnosis
Guaranteed result
The AI is sure
Apply chemical now
Treatment required immediately
Final expert result
```

## Required Chemical Treatment Warning

Whenever chemical treatment or fungicide/pesticide is mentioned, include:

```text
Ask a technician before applying chemical treatment, especially when many plants are affected.
```

## Healthy Result Safety Language

Do not say:

```text
Your plant is completely healthy.
```

Use:

```text
RiceCare AI did not detect strong visible symptoms in this image. Continue regular field monitoring.
```

# 18. Acceptance Checklist

Codex must verify:

```text
The app clearly says AI is advisory only.
The consent screen appears before main app use.
The result screen explains why the AI suggested the result.
Confidence score is visible.
Risk level is visible.
Alternative possible results are visible.
User can retake photo.
User can ask technician.
User can report wrong result.
User can save and delete results.
History includes audit trail.
Settings include accessibility controls.
High contrast mode visibly changes UI.
Large text mode visibly changes UI.
Privacy settings are available.
Low confidence results recommend retake or technician review.
High risk results include safety warning.
Chemical treatment advice includes technician warning.
No screen claims final diagnosis.
No screen removes farmer control.
No real backend or AI dependency is added.
```

# 19. Final Rule

RiceCare AI must be designed as a Human-Centered AI advisory tool.

The prototype should make the AI useful, but also limited, explainable, contestable, privacy-aware, accessible, and accountable.

The farmer remains the final decision-maker.
